Matheran

Aug 2002



On the way to Matheran village. It was terribly fogy with
very low visibility. The narrow gauge rail track
was put up by the British as they found an ideal retreat in Matheran.
Though it is much smaller than the track at Dehradoon,
it is none the less a feat of engineering.



Garbett Point on the way to Matheran village.

From Echo point



From Echo point - Lucas point at the left



Observe the smaller waterfalls... They were being completely
blown back due to the extremely stiff wind. It was difficult
even to keep your balance.



ummm... some gusts even blew part of the bigger waterfall up



Garbett point. Preparing to get down...



That rock face was slippery as hell. It was Vishu's first trek and
it was a tough ask for him. But he made it.



The great Matheran plateau rose above to our left.
Completely shrouded in clouds.



Fantastic conditions.
